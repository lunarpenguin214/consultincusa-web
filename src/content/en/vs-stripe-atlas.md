## When Stripe Atlas is the better choice

We're going to be honest with you. **Stripe Atlas is genuinely the right choice in three specific cases:**

### 1. You are building a venture-track tech startup
- You plan to raise from US VC funds within 2 years
- You want a Delaware C-Corp (the standard for VC-backed startups)
- You'll be issuing stock to founders and employees with 83(b) elections, ESOP, etc.
- The $2,500 Stripe credits + $50,000+ partner perks (AWS, Notion, OpenAI, etc.) actually matter to you because you'll use those tools at scale

If that's you, Stripe Atlas at $500 is a good deal. The Stripe brand carries weight with US investors, and the Atlas Community is full of similar founders. We will not pretend to be better for that case — we'd rather you go with Atlas and come to us later if you need anything they don't cover.

### 2. You only need Delaware
- Many tech founders default to Delaware regardless. If Delaware is your locked choice and you don't need Wyoming or any other state, Atlas is purpose-built for that.

### 3. You're already in the Stripe ecosystem and English-fluent
- If you process payments through Stripe, build software with Stripe primitives, and read English documentation easily, Atlas integrates seamlessly with your existing stack.

## When consultincusa is the better choice

### 1. You want any state OTHER than Delaware
Wyoming saves you $240/year in state franchise tax compared to Delaware. New Mexico saves even more (no annual report fee at all). Florida, Texas, Nevada — Atlas can't help you with any of these.

For most non-residents not raising VC, **Wyoming is the right choice** — and Atlas can't form Wyoming LLCs.

### 2. You want native-language support
Atlas is English-only. Their dashboard, support, documentation, community — all English. If you're a Brazilian founder, Mexican founder, Indian founder, Nigerian founder, or any other non-English-native, the Atlas experience is significantly harder.

We have a **Mercury rejection rescue protocol**: we route to Relay, Wise, or in-person traditional bank if you can travel. The 14-day guarantee covers this — we are not done until you have a US bank.
