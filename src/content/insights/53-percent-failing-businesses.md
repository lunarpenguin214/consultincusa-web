# What 53% of failing businesses have in common (it's not what consultants told you)

53% of US small business owners take home less than $30K a year.

Not the ones with no website. The ones with the LLC, the brand, the Stripe account, the Shopify store. The ones who told their parents they "started a business." Those operators. Most of them — broke.

## The jungle doesn't lie

Two kinds of hunters work the same forest.

The proactive ones lay bait. They study which trails their prey walks. They watch the water hole at dawn. They observe before they move.

The reactive ones ARE the bait. They keep moving, hoping to scare something into running their way. Or they sit and wait for prey to wander in. Sometimes it does. Mostly it doesn't.

Every day the jungle replaces parts of itself. New cubs born. Old animals taken. The chain doesn't stop. And here's the thing — almost nothing in the jungle goes to sleep hungry. The animals either find food, find a mate with food, or die trying. The food chain goes all in. Every single day.

Business doesn't.

Most operators are on the couch with WiFi while their competitors are at the watering hole.

## What the predator does that you don't

There's a body of research on what happens in a predator's brain right before a hunt. Lions, tigers, bears — same pattern. Neural activation lights up during the stalk that mirrors the actual kill. They're visualizing it. Seeing the leap. The takedown. The eat.

Olympic athletes do the same thing. Same brain regions fire during mental rehearsal as during the actual performance. Visualization builds the neural pathway. By the time the body acts, the brain has already done it a hundred times.

Here's the part most operators miss: **predators visualize without emotion overlay.**

The lion doesn't simulate the gazelle's terror. Just the leap. The trade. The eat.

The successful operator doesn't simulate the customer's awkwardness. Just the trade. The seeing. The exchange.

Most operators do the opposite. They emotionally simulate the buyer's discomfort and soften the ask. They emotionally simulate the work of reading their own books and skip it. They emotionally simulate the work of watching their competitors and assume their guess is enough.

Too nice on the hunt. Too tired in the gathering.

## The receipts are alive

Here's what nobody told you in any seminar:

**Every business that's beating yours is leaving public receipts.**

Their domain has DNS records. Their email has DKIM signatures. Their site footer has "Powered by" attributions. Their job postings list which tools they use. Their press coverage mentions their banks. Their case studies on vendor sites name their stack.

It's all public. It's all readable. It's all sitting there in plain text, in plain HTML, in plain RSS feeds.

And almost no one looks.

The jungle hunter reads paw prints, broken twigs, scent on the wind. The business hunter looks at the same kind of receipts — DNS, headers, public filings, tool fingerprints — and most of them refuse to read.

That's the 53%.

## What I saw in 30 minutes

Last week I looked at a $4M DTC brand. Public domain. Public footer. 30 minutes of looking.

Here's what their receipts told me:

- **3 different email tools** running simultaneously — Klaviyo, Sendlane, and Mailchimp. Combined: ~$1,800/month
- **Triple Whale installed but configured wrong** — visible from script tag placement that breaks attribution before the click fires
- **Two SMS providers double-charging** — Klaviyo SMS AND Postscript, both pointed at the same subscriber list
- **Footer still says "Powered by [old platform]"** from a migration 6 months ago — meaning their old DNS records, email reputation, and SEO authority are split between two domains

I added it up. **$4,800/month in pure waste. $57,600/year.**

The operator running that brand probably thinks she's smart with her stack. She isn't dumb. She's just not reading.

Her competitors are.

## Why the 47% above the line operate differently

You know what I notice about operators who take home more than $100K a year?

They look at things.

That's it. That's the whole secret.

They look at their books once a week, not once a quarter. They look at their competitors' websites once a month. They look at their customer service tickets once a day. They look at their bank statements line by line.

They have not done anything fancy. They just LOOK.

The 53% don't look. They guess. They hope. They emotionally simulate what they'd see if they looked, decide they don't want to see it, and move on.

The lion looks. The gazelle looks. Both look at exactly what's there. Neither one looks for what they wish were there.

You can be a lion. Or a gazelle. Or a couch.

The 53% picked couch.

## How to read your own receipts in 60 minutes

You don't need our help to start. You can do this tonight.

**1. Your own DNS** — Type `dig yourdomain.com MX` into Terminal (or use mxtoolbox.com). Look at where your email is routing. Is it where you think it is?

**2. Your own footer** — View source on your website. Search for "Powered by". What does it say? Does it match what you think your stack is?

**3. One competitor's footer** — Pick the brand most adjacent to yours. View source. What's in their footer? What scripts load? What's their email/SMS setup?

**4. One competitor's job posting** — LinkedIn. Search "[Competitor name] Klaviyo" or "[Their domain] HubSpot." What tools are listed as required on their last 5 job descriptions?

**5. Your bank statements** — Last 90 days. Sort by vendor. Top 10 most-paid vendors. Do they map to revenue? Or are 4 of them tools you forgot you signed up for?

**6. Your subscription receipts in your email** — Search Gmail for "subscription confirmation" or "your monthly bill" or "auto-renewed." What's running on autopay you forgot about?

**7. Your competitors' press releases** — Crunchbase or Google. Last 12 months of news. Funding round language tells you their stage. Hire announcements tell you their direction. Partnerships tell you their stack.

That's 60 minutes. That's the entire 53%-vs-47% difference.

You will see things. Things that will make your jaw clench.

Some you can fix tonight.

Others you'll need help with.
